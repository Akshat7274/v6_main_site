  • Robotronics

A special day for the robot enthusiasts on this day we will be having fun games, competitions, exhibitions and workshops. This event will have 3 phases starting with (ROBOFUN) in which the participants will be building their robots according to the given problem statement and then the robots will compete with each other with different games.

Then comes phase 2(ROBOFEST) in which the participants will present their robots to the audience and will pitch for funds so that they get the “power of selling” and the audience will get to see the Tech innovations.

Last but not the least phase 3 (ROBOCLASS) in which the students who want to pursue their career in AI or if they want to know more about the robots or their future in Robo class the audience will get a chance to use the robot.

Phase-1

  1. The participant would be given a problem statement a day before in which they have to design the robot on the basis of the problem statement. The problem statement will be revealed on the official Instagram page of siNUsoid.
  2. After the robots are prepared, they have to bring them and compete with the other participants' robots.
In this, there will be 2 games.

  1. Robo-soccer

    Robot Soccer is a robot sport, where robot machines compete against each other in a variation of the game of soccer. In robot soccer, each team is fully autonomous - once the game starts the only input from any human is from the referee.

    Rules

    • The team can have a maximum number of 1 offline and 2 online players
    • Only one participant(An offline player) should control the robot; no player can be exchanged in the middle of a match.
    • A robot can push or hit the ball. It cannot withhold or grab the ball.
    • Human interference (e.g. touching the robot) during the game is not allowed.
    • Human interference (e.g. touching the robot) during the game is not allowed.
    • Coordinators reserve the right to ask for an explanation of the robot

    Point System

    • Total Points = 40
    • Point System for Robot Soccer match
    • 2 Rounds each of 2 mins with a gap of 1 min for planning
    • 1 goal=10 points
    • Outside the marked area will lead to -5 points
    • Point system for virtual presentation
    • Each point mentioned will give 4 marks, which makes it a total of 24 marks for the problem statement.
    • Presentation and speaking contain 6 marks.

  2. Roborace

    It is also a robot sports game, where robot machines compete against each other in a variation of the game of race. In robot race, once the game starts the only input from any human is from the race officials.

    Rules

    • A robot cannot hit the other robot. It can overtake it
    • Human interference (e.g. touching the robot) during the game is not allowed No more AC/DC power supply will be provided at the sight of the play. Coordinators reserve the right to ask for an explanation of the robot.

    Point System

    • Total Points = 40
    • 2 laps each of 2 mins
    • 1 Lap complete =10 points
    • Hitting the robot will lead to -5 points
    • Hitting the robot will lead to -5 points
    • Each point mentioned will give 4 marks, which makes it a total of 24 marks for the problem statement.
    • Presentation and speaking contain 6 marks.

Phase-2(Robo-class)

  1. The “ROBOCLASS” will be organised to give some deeper knowledge about robots and to give knowledge about the future of Artificial Intelligence. It will help many students and will work as a career guide for them.
  2. The student who attends the workshop will be given a chance to use the robot.
  3. The participant's robots will also present their robots to the students and get feedback from the students and administration.
  4. The participant with the best feedback will get some great prizes and a special mention in aNUkriti.
