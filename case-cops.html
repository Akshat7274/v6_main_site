Dress Code : Business Attire for Case Competition

In this, students will analyse a given case study and they will be marked on the basis of their assessment and how they perform during the question-answer round.

The students are required to analyse the case to determine the strategic vision and present a recommendation on the actions the company should take to improve, sustain, turn around, or correct its performance. The analysis should include the highlights of a strategic plan for the next three to five years. The teams are assessed on their realistic recognition of the problem(s), their assessment of alternatives, their recommendations for action, and their ability to respond to the Judges’ questions


Rules and Regulations

  • Each participant can form a team of two to three members in each division. A person cannot be a member of more than one team. Division definitions follow
  • Each team member must be on record with the siNUsoid- The Tech Fest of NIIT University.
  • Student teams agree that they will not seek outside help from any source after receiving the case. It is not acceptable to ask specific or general questions related to the case of anyone or to have anyone read, listen to, watch, or provide feedback on the presentation. The presenting team is charged with doing its own work on the case with no assistance from anyone not on the team. It is NOT permissible to contact the case authors.
  • All deadlines below must be met and plagiarism won’t be tolerated.
  • Chapters wishing to receive case materials should submit the Preliminary Registration Form by the date declared.
  • Case materials will be provided to teams that have completed the Preliminary Registration Form
  • The presentation and Q/A session will last up to 10 minutes. Presentations will be limited to 7 minutes. Judges will have the remaining time (typically 3-5 minutes) to ask questions.
  • Each team may select the media that will most effectively deliver its message. However, two caveats apply. First, our university will provide only the following support equipment: tables, chairs, a lectern, an LCD projector and a screen. Teams must bring their own computers, computer cables, extension cords, etc. The LCD provided by our university has the following connections – VGA, USB, and HDMI. Second, it will NOT be accepted for teams to record (audio, movie, etc.) their presentation and show it to the judges. Teams should have other plans if an LCD projector does not work.
  • Advisors and Coaches are not team members.
  • If teams use a PowerPoint presentation, a PowerPoint handout of the slide presentation must be provided. No additional written material is necessary. The Judges’ evaluations are based on presentations only. (Printing services will not be provided by Sinusoid).
  • Bonus points for teams who do role-play along with the presentation.
  • Presentations will be closed to the public. Each team may, at their discretion, have two designated “listeners” attend their presentation. The designated listeners will not be permitted to ask questions, make comments, or provide direction of any kind to the team. That may result in disqualifying the team.
  • Each team must certify that all work on the case and presentation was completed by those presenting the case and no others.
  • Teams should be fully equipped for presentation; no technical support will be provided
